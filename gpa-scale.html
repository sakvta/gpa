Calculating GPA

The GPA is calculated by taking the grade points earned in each course, multiplying them by the number of credits the course is worth, adding all those products together, and then dividing by the total number of credits taken.

Example Calculation

For example, if a student has three courses with grades A (4.0), B (3.0), and C (2.0) in 3-credit courses:

GPA = (4.0 × 3) + (3.0 × 3) + (2.0 × 3) ÷ (3 + 3 + 3) = 3.0

Different GPA Scales

While the 4.0 scale is the most common, some schools use weighted scales to give extra points for honors, Advanced Placement (AP), or International Baccalaureate (IB) courses. These scales may go up to 5.0 or even 6.0.

Importance of GPA

The GPA is important for several reasons:

  • College Admissions: GPA is a key factor in college admissions decisions.
  • Scholarships and Honors: Many scholarships and academic honors are based on GPA.
  • Career Opportunities: Employers may also consider GPA when hiring recent graduates.
