How to Use the College GPA Calculator

To calculate your GPA, follow these steps:

  1. Enter the name of your course in the "Subject name " field.
  2. Enter the credit hours for the course in the "Credit" field (default value is set to 3).
  3. Enter your grade for the course in the "Grade" field.
  4. If you have multiple courses, click "Add" to add additional fields for each course.
  5. Once you've entered all your courses, click "Calculate GPA" to calculate your weighted GPA.

How to Use GPA Raise Calculation

The GPA Raise Calculator on this website helps users determine their new GPA after raising their existing GPA by a certain number of points. Here's how it works step by step:

  1. Current GPA: Users input their current GPA in the "Current GPA" field.
  2. Current Credits: Users input the number of current credits they have earned in the "Current Credits" field.
  3. Credits this semester: Users input the number of credits they plan to take this semester in the "I am taking credits this semester" field.
  4. Raise number: Users input the number of GPA points they want to raise in the "If I want to raise my GPA points" field.
  5. Calculate Raised GPA Button: When users click the "Calculate Raised GPA" button, the calculator performs the following calculations:
    • Calculates the product of the current GPA and current credits.
    • Adds the raised GPA points to the current GPA.
    • Calculates the total credits by adding the current credits and the credits for the semester.
    • Calculates the new GPA by dividing the product of the raised GPA and total credits by the new total credits.
  6. Result Display: The calculator then displays the calculated raised GPA in the "Result" section below the form.
